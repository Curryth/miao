Jisoo，日语：キム・ジス，1995年1月3日－）是一名韩国歌手与演员。她以艺名Jisoo（韩语：지수，日语：ジス，中文：智秀）为人熟知。现为YG娱乐旗下女子音乐组合BLACKPINK成员之一 2011年－2015年：出道前 Jisoo早于2011年在YG娱乐年末选拔中被其经纪公司代表梁铉锡选中，成为YG旗下的一名练习生，而继同组合成员Jennie在2012年4月10日率先通过官方博客登载后，YG娱乐也在同年5月4日首次公开Jisoo的黑白照，然而与Jennie一样并未透露任何资料。[6] 2013年1月15日及1月19日，YG娱乐再度公开Jisoo的照片及其个人资料，并且表明有计划让新女团于2013年上半季出道，然而至一月底，新女团仍未有出道消息，官方释出相关资讯的动作也暂时到此为止。 在正式出道前，Jisoo已活跃于音乐录影带、电视剧及广告等领域的演出与代言，包括参演所属经纪公司旗下艺人Epik High在2014年10月发行的第八张正规专辑《Shoebox》主打歌之一《Spoiler》的音乐录影带片中女主角[7]，同年11月11日，YG娱乐推出限定组合HI秀贤，其发行的单曲《我不一样》音乐录影带在18日公开，无预警揭露Jisoo再次担任了此音乐录影带中的配角出演。[8] 2015年，Jisoo开始大量的在幕前曝光，陆续代言相机、手机、校服、手机游戏等多项产品，较著名的是与李敏镐一同代言的休闲包品牌“新秀丽”[9]，以及与当时即将出道的同经纪公司组合iKON共同为校服品牌“Smart Uniform Korea”代言[10]。同年5月首度参与电视剧作品，以客串身份出演KBS的电视剧《制作人》，饰演综艺节目《两天一夜》的新成员，至此，Jisoo已逐渐为大众所熟知。[11] 2016年至今：BLACKPINK 主条目：BLACKPINK 2016年6月，在随着YG娱乐接连公布新女团成员Jennie、Lisa后，Jisoo在6月15日公开，成为BLACKPINK第三位成员[12]，随后于同年8月8日作为队内门面及领唱担当正式出道[13]，并随组合发行首张单曲专辑《Square One》，主打曲为《口哨》及《Boombayah》[14]。出道后，Jisoo也开始陆续活跃于主持的工作，首次主持是在9月4日为TWICE成员定延代班，与孔昇延、金珉硕携手主持SBS旗下音乐节目《人气歌谣》[15]，而在2017年2月5日开始正式和GOT7成员珍荣、与NCT成员道英成为该节目固定主持人，直至2018年2月4日结束该节目主持工作。[16][17] 2018年7月担任《意外的Q》嘉宾，透露她面试的歌曲是李恩美《我有爱人了》，与其他面试者不同，因为该曲是抒情歌，而不是嘻哈或饶舌歌。她也自行跟制作单位洽谈出席节目，而没有经过官方安排。 Early life Kim Ji-soo was born on January 3, 1995[2][3] in Gunpo, Gyeonggi Province, South Korea.[4][failed verification][5] She has an older brother and sister.[6][7] As a child, she played basketball and practiced Taekwondo.[8] She was a fan of the Korean boy group TVXQ.[8] Jisoo attended high school at the School of Performing Arts Seoul;[2] in 11th grade, she founded a drama club at the school and earned more experience in the entertainment industry by attending auditions.[9] 2011–2016: Career beginnings and debut with Blackpink Main article: Blackpink In 2011, Jisoo joined YG Entertainment as a trainee after passing its auditions.[10] In October 2014, she appeared in Epik High's music video for "Spoiler + Happen Ending" as a heartbroken girl.[11] The same year, she starred in Hi Suhyun's music video for "I'm Different"[12] as Bobby's girlfriend. In 2015, Jisoo made a cameo appearance in the KBS2 drama The Producers with labelmates Sandara Park of 2NE1 and Kang Seung-yoon of Winner.[13] Jisoo debuted as one of the oldest of the four members of girl group Blackpink on August 8, 2016, alongside Jennie, Rosé and Lisa, with the release of their single album Square One.[14] 2017–present: Solo endeavors and acting From 2017 to 2018, Jisoo joined Inkigayo as a host alongside Got7's Jin-young and NCT's Doyoung.[15] She first ventured into acting in 2019 with a short cameo appearance in the tvN fantasy drama Arthdal Chronicles.[16] In 2020, Jisoo co-wrote the lead single "Lovesick Girls" of Blackpink's first Korean-language studio album The Album.[17] On August 18, 2020 YG Entertainment confirmed Jisoo would star in her first role as a lead actress in the JTBC drama Snowdrop[18] alongside actor Jung Hae-in.[19] The series aired from December 18, 2021, to January 30, 2022, on JTBC domestically and on Disney+ internationally. Despite the controversies related to the alleged historical distortions by the show, Jisoo's acting was positively received by critics, who stated that she "certainly proves her mettle" and that her "range is commendable".[20] YG Entertainment confirmed that Jisoo's debut as a solo artist was in the works but without a date yet due to her commitments to filming Snowdrop. She will be the last member of Blackpink to make her solo debut.[21]
